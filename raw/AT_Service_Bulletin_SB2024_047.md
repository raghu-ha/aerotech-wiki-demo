# AeroTech Industries — Service Bulletin SB-2024-047

**Document ID:** SB-2024-047  
**Applies To:** Titan 900 Series Engines  
**Subject:** Fan Blade Leading Edge Inspection and Replacement  
**Date Issued:** March 2024  
**Revision:** Rev 2.0  
**Priority:** MANDATORY — Within 300 flight cycles

---

## 1. Reason for Issue

AeroTech has identified accelerated erosion on fan blade leading edges (AT-T900-FB-001 through AT-T900-FB-022) on engines operating in high-particulate environments.

Inspections on 45 engines revealed:
- 22% showed erosion exceeding 3mm before the 500-cycle interval
- 5% exceeded the 5mm replacement threshold before 400 cycles
- One engine showed asymmetric erosion causing vibration increase (Zone 1: 4.1 IPS vs limit 5.0 IPS)

## 3. Failure Progression

```
Stage 1 (0–300 cycles):
  Minor pitting on leading edge
  No performance impact

Stage 2 (300–450 cycles):
  Erosion exceeds 3mm chord loss
  Fuel burn increase: 0.3–0.8%
  EGT margin reduction: 5–12°C

Stage 3 (450+ cycles):
  Erosion exceeds 5mm → MANDATORY REPLACEMENT
  Risk of blade fragment separation
  Vibration increase in Zone 1
```

---

## 4. Required Actions

### 4.1 Immediate (within 50 flight cycles)
Enhanced visual inspection of all fan blades per AT-AMM-T900-72-11-01.

**Rejection Criteria:**
- Chord loss exceeding 2mm
- Delamination at titanium-composite bond
- Any asymmetric erosion pattern

### 4.2 Compliance (within 300 flight cycles)
Replace all fan blades with improved specification:
- **Replacement Part:** AT-T900-FB-002 (Rev B)
- **New Inspection Interval:** 750 cycles (extended from 500)
- **Labour Estimate:** 46 man-hours per engine

### 4.3 Post-Replacement Checks
1. Ground engine run per AT-AMM-T900-71-00-01
2. Zone 1 vibration — must be below 3.5 IPS
3. EGT margin at takeoff — minimum 45°C required
4. ACMS baseline reset
